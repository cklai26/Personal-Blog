A new journey of an architect

Mar 24th 2023 7 min read

As an architect, I have always been fascinated by the interplay of form and function. I love creating spaces that are not only beautiful but also practical, spaces that people can inhabit and interact with. But after years of designing buildings and structures, I began to feel restless. I was ready for a new challenge, something that would allow me to explore different aspects of design and creativity.

That's when I discovered UI/UX design, a field that combined my passion for aesthetics with a user-centered approach to design. It was a perfect fit for me. As I delved deeper into the field, I realized that I could use my skills as an architect to create intuitive and engaging digital experiences for users. I was excited to embark on this new journey and eager to see where it would take me.

At first, the transition from architecture to UI/UX design was challenging. I had to learn new tools and techniques, and I had to adjust my way of thinking to focus on digital products rather than physical spaces. But I quickly adapted, drawing on my experience as an architect to create designs that were not only visually appealing but also functional and easy to use.

Unlike architecture, UI/UX design allows me to work on smaller, more focused projects with quicker turnaround times.

One of the things that I enjoy most about UI/UX design is the opportunity to work on a variety of projects. Unlike architecture, which often involves large-scale projects that take years to complete, UI/UX design allows me to work on smaller, more focused projects with quicker turnaround times. I love the challenge of coming up with creative solutions to design problems and the satisfaction of seeing my work come to life..

As I continue on my new journey, I find that my background in architecture gives me a unique perspective on UI/UX design. I am able to approach design problems from a different angle, considering not just how the product looks and functions, but also how it fits into the larger context of the user's life. I believe that my experience as an architect gives me a deeper understanding of how design can shape and influence human behavior.

Now, several years into my new career, I couldn't be happier with my decision to transition to UI/UX design. I have found a new passion and a new way to apply my skills as an architect. I enjoy the fast-paced, ever-changing nature of the field and the opportunity to work with a variety of clients and industries. As I look back on my journey, I realize that sometimes taking a leap of faith and trying something new can lead to unexpected and fulfilling opportunities.
